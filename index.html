About this Dataset


This dataset comprises of 216 sequences, 3888000 frames (approx. 36 hours) collected from the a cross-lingual communication study from 36 pairs in 2 language groups.

This dataset contains 5 different types of data. All data are collect in a cross-lingual communication lab study and collect verbal data, ideas and gestures performed by each participants. Each group performing 10 minutes skeleton data, for a total of 216 sequences, 3888000 frames.

Each pair contains 2 people, with two kinds of language group: native speaker vs. non-native speaker (EL1-EL2) and both non-native speaker (EL2-EL2)) performing 4 kinds of gestures (iconic, metaphoric, pointing and non-iconic).

Experiment Design


We recruited 64 naïve subjects to participate in our study, grouped into 2 main groups (EL1-EL2: native speaker vs. non-native speaker & EL2-EL2: both non-native speakers), each main group contains 16 pairs.

Each pair was brought to laboratory and was asked to discuss three brainstorming tasks (below) with three different conditions (audio, face-to-face, HandVis).






Brainstorming Task


The "Extra Eye","Extra Thumb" and "Extra Wings" brainstorming task used in our study. For example, participants were asked, "What are the benefits and difficulties if people had an third eye in the future?" for the task "Extra Eye".
